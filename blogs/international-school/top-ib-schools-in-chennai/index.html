IB Pedagogy

The IB Learning Approach

The International Baccalaureate framework is built on a philosophy that goes far beyond grades and examinations. It is a holistic system designed to develop the whole child — intellectually, emotionally, and socially.

1

Inquiry-Based Education

Students drive their own learning through questions, investigation, and discovery — developing a lifelong love for learning that extends beyond the classroom.

2

Project-Based Learning

Real-world projects connect academic concepts to practical applications, building collaboration, problem-solving, and presentation skills alongside subject knowledge.

3

Global Curriculum Framework

The IB curriculum provides an internationally consistent framework that allows students to connect local contexts with global issues and perspectives.

4

Creativity & Independent Thinking

The curriculum celebrates creative expression and individual thought, encouraging students to develop their unique voice and approach to problem-solving.
